Caltech FlyTracker


If you use the FlyTracker for your publication, please cite the following:
Detailed manuscript is currently in preparation, meanwhile, you can refer to our ECCV 2014 paper, Detecting social actions of fruit flies, where we briefly describe the tracking and feature extraction mechanism of the tracker.

People: FlyTracker was developed by Eyrun Eyjolfsdottir and Michael Maire, in the Computational Vision Lab at Caltech, under the supervision of Pietro Perona. Kristin Branson provided support for JAABA (Janelia Automatic Animal Behavior Annotator) compatibility and ufmf readability. Conchi Fernandez helped implement the visualizer tool. During development we received several videos and invaluable feedback from members of the David Anderson Lab at Caltech, particularly from Eric Hoopfer and Kenta Asahina. Other people who tested the tracker and provided videos and feedback include: Rod Lim, Brian Duistermars, Jonathan Schor, Kiichi Watanabe, Catherine Schretter, Miguel Gaspar, and Jonathan Massey.
